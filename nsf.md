**Challenge 1: AI for Substance Abuse Risk Detection from Social Signals**
The Mission
Substance abuse is a growing societal crisis, and early warning signals often emerge in digital
environments such as social media and online communities.
In this challenge, you will design an AI-driven system that identifies risk signals related to alcohol and
drug abuse from public, anonymized data. The goal is to transform unstructured data into interpretable,
actionable insights that support early awareness and intervention.
Datasets (Provided or Recommended)
• Instructor-provided curated social media dataset (anonymized forum discussions)
• CDC overdose and public health data: https://data.cdc.gov/
• NIDA data: https://nida.nih.gov/research-topics/trends-statistics
• Kaggle dataset: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/jessicali9530/kuc-hackathon-winter-2018
• Optional additional datasets (with approval)
Core Tasks
1. Risk Signal Detection
Identify:
• Substance use mentions
• Emotional distress or relapse signals
Expanded Expectations:
• Develop a robust pipeline to extract relevant textual signals from noisy, real-world data
• Design prompts or fine-tuning strategies for LLMs that capture nuanced language (e.g., indirect
references, slang, emotional tone)
• Compare multiple approaches (e.g., rule-based vs embedding-based vs LLM-based) and analyze
their performance
Using:
• Large language model prompting or fine-tuning
• Embedding-based classification or retrieval
2. Temporal and Behavioral Analysis
Detect:
• Spikes and trends
• Evolving narratives
Expanded Expectations:
• Model temporal dynamics of risk signals (e.g., sudden spikes, gradual increases, recurring
patterns)
• Cluster users/posts/topics to uncover behavioral patterns and emerging themes
• Analyze how discussions evolve over time and identify potential early-warning indicators
Using:
• Embedding similarity or clustering
• Time-aware analysis
3. Explainability and Reasoning
Provide:
• Supporting evidence
• Summaries
• Interpretable outputs
Expanded Expectations:
• Generate explanations that justify model predictions using retrieved evidence or attention patterns
• Provide concise summaries for decision-makers (e.g., public health analysts)
• Ensure outputs are transparent, auditable, and aligned with ethical AI principles
Innovation Tracks (Select One)
Track A: AI Modeling and Reasoning
Focus on building a strong AI pipeline:
• Large language model-based classification
• Retrieval-augmented generation
• Embedding-based pipelines
• Explainable outputs
Track B: Data Intelligence and Decision Support
Focus on insights and system development:
